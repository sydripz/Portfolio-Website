Olive

Olive is a socially based workout creation and sharing platform. Olive creates a space for users to publish their own workouts while engaging with their friends workouts through saving, sharing and commenting on the content.

My Process

Reasearch

Before beginning my design work, I knew that clarification of the health and fitness industry market as well as user reserarch was foundational to creating a valuable product.

I found that most fitness apps were tied to a certain influencer or company, but there were few in the marketplace that prioritized the ability for users to create and elevate their workout content.

Some fitness apps like Strava allow users to create and guide the content of the app... but a gym specific, user populated app seems to have been missed within the marketplace. Instead, gym-goers have to navigate between multiple platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube to find and compile workouts.

Ideation

Olive is a workout app specific to the gym & weighlifting, and the user personas created after analyzing the research refelct the diversity within this environment. I created three user personas to represent the fitness influencer with years of experience, the gym newbie, and the typical gym-bro type.

User flows were vital to helping shape the flows of key features within the app. The creation of user journeys worked in tandem to help identify how to create the most intuitive and fluid journey for a user to take to accomplish a goal.

Wireframing & Prototyping

I utilized card sorting with a variety of participants to help better understand how users would naturally group features of the app. The key categories that emerged between the participants were activity, profile, feed and training.

The grouping patterns revealed through the card sort were a valuable starting point for wireframes. The initial mid-fidelity wireframes presented a main navigation bar with five different icons including home, explore, feed, training and profile.

This structure was designed to have a social platform feel and appearance that works in tandem with the fitness features such as creating, editing, and sharing workouts.

Testing

After the mid-fidelity prototypes were completed, it was time to put the app through multiple usability tests. This is one of my most valued tools and steps in the design proces, as it provides new clarification and direction within the app. The usability test had three main tasks that would help identity the ituititiveness and functionality of the wireframes.

The three tasks were to: Create a workout schedule, Find a workout to follow, and Write a workout.

Utilizing a rainbow spreadsheet helped identify and analyze points of friction, observation, and positivie&negative quotes. Organizing the data in this way helped me make an informed decision on what to prioritize when beginning iterations and redesigns within the app.

I was able to prioritize key adjustments after analyzing the rainbow spreadsheet. Some of the top priorities included users needing a clear way to review and finalize their workout, and an ability to save & locate their friends workouts. The results showed a need to clarify elements like "stories" while providiing a defined & direct focus to the app.

Iteration

After analyzing the feedback from user tests, I spent considerable time iterating and refining the design. Throughout this stage, I took the feedback I received and honed in on the core features and needs of the target audience.

This included combining the features of the "Home" and "Feed" screens into an updated Home screen, and creating congruent UI across all screens. These intentions helped refine the app so that it served the needs of users better while creating distinction from other social platforms. This helped ensure that the app brings intentional value to gaps missing in the workout app market.

Additional iterations from user fedback involved adding color distinction and icons to help quickly identissy workout categories. Multiple interview particpants suggested that illustrations would be more effective at quickly identifying workout categories rather than having a text dominant screen.

The focus of the Training screen remained focused on organizing content. The user can toggle between "wokouts, create & progess" screens to easily view workout information, create new workouts or view their progress. While the Training section of the app contains a wealth of user information, its key focus was data organization abd user control & access to information.

The users profile page is designed to showcase their workouts and posts for the Olive community to see. The profile page is designed to have a distinct social platform feel, while promoting the specific workout focus of the app. Users can control the aesthetic of their feed while highlighting workouts for others to view and try. A public workout inventory is also displayed when a user toggles to the wieght icon. This is a users public display to their community, and it is designed to highlight the specific user and the workouts they have performed.

Conclusion

Utilizing research, ideation, wireframing & prototyping, testing and iteration I created a workout focused app centered around the ability for users to share, engage and collaborate.
