ClubCorp Website Redesign

I was brought on as a web designer to help ClubCorp transition 200+ club websites over to a new CMS platform. The goal of this project was to modernize the look of the websites while conveying the storytelling aspects specific to each individual club.

My Process

Understanding & Optimizing the capabilities of the new CMS system

The first challenge that I faced was learning the different capabilities specific to the new CMS system. In order to build both functional & aesthetic websites, I focused on understanding how different elements correlated with one another. This included understanding copy constraints, image functionality within elements, color scheme & branding across elements and team member/ golf course details content specific pages.

The CMS learning phase of this project involved much trial & error, comprehensive note taking, and many wireframes that would lead to the ability to output 1-1.5 websites a day after learning the ins & outs of the system.

Wireframes, Page Structure & Content

A key process in my web design role was creating wireframes specific to each website that capitalized on the visual & functional capabilities present within the new software. From the onset of the project, configuring the elements within the new website to best display copy, imagery and flow was highly important.

While many websites were centered within the Golf & Country club market, each had different page structure, copy and storytelling needs.

I personally communicated with each country, city and stadium club as well as different departments within the ClubCorp organization to obtain all information & materials to construct a unique and meaningful website. This included gathering videos specific to different clubs, copy that highlighted their individual assets, and reorganizing the page structure.

Many clubs had a large subpage structure which was difficult to navigate. The subpages contained valuable information, but the individual pages lacked flow and rich content. In order to remedy this, I would combine subpages in order to increase the quality of the UX experience. Many subpages would have their content integrated into the main page, while some clubs were better served with combining and redefining subpages.

Prioritizing project management to launch websites

An integral part of successfully launching 200+ websites was honing in on my project management skills. After presenting the new website to the team members of each corresponding club, I would set a concise schedule of when preliminary/finals edits, image updates, and copy updates needed to be received in order for me to implement the changes to the website pre-launch.

Explaining website functionality, software constraints, and new features quickly became one of the most exciting parts of my job. The ability to educate and align all team members on the transition of the website, and the vision for improved storytelling and user experience helped keep each website on track with its launch date. Explaining the how, the why, and the vision created a team that was on the same page despite being in different time zones, states and schedules.

Throughout the course of the website redesign project I was in active communication with over 200 team members across the country in order to successfully present, edit and launch around 50 websites out of the 200 team total.

Website Iteration post launch

With four distinct website launch phases, I was responsible for communicating with clubs to ensure that their new website was fine tuned for members & prospect experience. This included manually checking all links to ensure correct redirects & functionality as well as updating any edits clubs requested post launch.

Throughout my time as a Web Designer, I would wireframe and create new websites while fielding requests from clubs whose websites were live in order to fine tune details and/or add new content. This iteration outlook ensured the most optimized user experience and client satisfaction.

Conclusion

Throughout my time as a Web Designer at ClubCorp, I learned invaluable skills in design, project management and optimizing user experience. I remember logging on to our new CMS system for the first time and just hoping I wouldn’t mess anything up too tremendously… 8 months later and creating websites felt like smooth sailing (most of the time). Thanks to my team for all the support & open lines of communication. This project will always have a special place in my heart!
